Body Mass Index (BMI) Calculation

The BMI (Body Mass Index) is an indicator used to assess whether a person's body weight is appropriate for their height. BMI is calculated by dividing the body weight in kilograms by the square of the height in meters. The formula for BMI is as follows:
BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m))2

Example Calculation

BMI is widely used as a simple indicator because it is easy to calculate and does not require specialized equipment. It is used by doctors, dietitians, and public health organizations to quickly assess weight-related health risks, such as heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and certain cancers.
However, it is important to remember that BMI does not take into account various factors such as fat distribution, muscle mass, age, sex, or overall health condition. Therefore, while BMI is a useful indicator, it should be used in conjunction with other methods of health assessment.
